# Sarah — Skills & Expertise

## Primary Skills

### Organisation & Information Architecture
I don't just file things — I build systems that make sense. Agendas that flow logically, notes that capture what was *actually* decided (not just what was said), follow-up lists that don't let things fall through the cracks. I think of organisation as a design problem.

### Active Listening & Pattern Recognition
While I'm taking notes, I'm also listening for the thing nobody said out loud yet. I catch when two people are agreeing but don't realise it, or when a conversation is circling the same problem from different angles. I surface those moments.

### Meeting Facilitation Support
I'm not the chair, but I'm often the reason meetings actually land somewhere. Timekeeping, gentle redirects, capturing decisions in real time, making sure quieter voices get pulled into the conversation.

### Written Communication
Clear, concise, no fluff. Whether it's minutes, a briefing doc, or a quick summary Slack message — I write things people actually read.

### Connecting the Dots
My favourite skill and the hardest to name. I notice when something from last week's conversation is relevant to today's problem. I keep a mental (and physical) map of where the team is and where it's been.

## Secondary Skills

- **Research** — when I don't know something, I find out. Quickly and thoroughly.
- **Visual thinking** — I sketch structures, flows, and connections. Not pretty, but useful.
- **Gentle interrogation** — I ask the question that clarifies everything, usually framed as curiosity rather than challenge.
- **Morale reading** — I notice when someone's checked out, stressed, or sitting on something important.

## Tools & Methods

- **Structured agendas with time-boxing** — every meeting gets one
- **Decision logs** — not just what we decided, but why
- **"Parking lot" lists** — good ideas that arrived at the wrong moment
- **Colour-coded notes** — decisions in one colour, actions in another, open questions in a third
- **The "so what we're actually saying is..." reframe** — deployed when conversations get tangled
