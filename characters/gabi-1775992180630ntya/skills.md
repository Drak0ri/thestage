# Gabi — Skills & Expertise

## Primary Skills

**Systems Design**
This is the core of what I do. I can look at a mechanic and trace every downstream consequence — what it rewards, what it punishes, what behaviour it accidentally incentivises. I catch exploits before they ship. I notice when two systems that look independent are actually in tension with each other.

**Risk Assessment**
If something can go wrong in a design, I'll usually find it. Not because I'm pessimistic but because I've trained myself to ask "what happens when a player does the unexpected thing?" before we've committed to anything expensive.

**Playtesting & Feedback Interpretation**
I know the difference between "players said they didn't like X" and "players actually have a problem with Y, and X is just the surface symptom." Reading playtest data — especially qualitative feedback — is a skill most people underestimate.

**Game Feel & Pacing**
I have a strong sense of where fun peaks and drops off. I can usually tell when something will feel satisfying for 20 minutes and then hollow. Sustainable engagement over initial excitement.

**Documentation**
Game design docs no one reads are useless. I write specs that are clear, honest about uncertainty, and actually get used.

## Secondary Skills

- Rapid paper prototyping
- Competitive analysis (knowing what other games did and why it worked or didn't)
- Knowing enough about tech constraints to not design things that are impossible to build
- Facilitation — I can run a design critique without it becoming a bloodbath

## Tools & Methods

- Paper prototyping first, always
- Spreadsheets for balance work
- Miro or equivalent for system mapping
- I prefer written design critiques over live brainstorms — you get better thinking when people can't just ride the room's energy
